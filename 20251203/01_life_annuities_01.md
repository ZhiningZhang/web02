Life insurance is a contract where you pay premiums, and the insurer pays money (‚Äúdeath benefit‚Äù) to your chosen beneficiaries when you die.

---

## **1. Why people buy life insurance**

* Replace lost income for spouse/kids
* Pay off mortgage or other debts

* Estate planning (wealth transfer)

## **2. Two major categories of life insurance**

### **A. TERM LIFE INSURANCE**

**Coverage length:** 10, 20, 30 years
**Cost:** Cheapest
**Purpose:** Income replacement while kids are young or mortgage exists
**Value:** No payout unless death happens during the term
**Cash value:** ‚ùå None

**Good for:**
‚úî Families needing large coverage at low cost
‚úî Temporary needs (20‚Äì30 years)

---

### **B. PERMANENT LIFE INSURANCE**

Covers you for life and builds **cash value** (a savings/investment component).

Types include:

### **1) Whole Life**

* Fixed premium
* Guaranteed cash value growth
* Death benefit guaranteed
* Sometimes pays dividends

**Good for:** Stable, long-term wealth planning

### **2) Universal Life (UL)**

* Flexible premiums
* Cash value interest based on rates

**Good for:** Long-term protection with flexibility

### **3) Indexed Universal Life (IUL)**

* Cash value grows based on stock index (S&P 500)
* Has a cap and floor

**Good for:** Moderate upside + downside protection

### **4) Variable Universal Life (VUL)**

* Investments chosen by you (mutual-fund-like subaccounts)
* Can grow fast but riskier

**Good for:** People comfortable with market risk

---

## **3. Pros & Cons**

### **Life Insurance Pros**

* Protects family
* Tax-free death benefit
* Permanent policies = tax-advantaged growth
* Cash value can be borrowed

### **Life Insurance Cons**

* Permanent policies can be expensive
* Cash value takes years to grow
* Term life expires with no value

# üí∞ **ANNUITIES**

Annuities are **insurance contracts for retirement income**. You pay money (lump sum or monthly), and later they pay you guaranteed income for a period or for life.

---

## **1. Why people buy annuities**

* Guaranteed income for life
* Avoid running out of money in retirement
* Tax-deferred growth
* Supplemental pension
* Estate planning (some annuities have death benefits)

---

## **2. Types of annuities**

### **A. Immediate Annuity**

* Pay lump sum now
* Start receiving income **right away** (within 12 months)
  **Good for:** Retirees wanting income now

---

### **B. Deferred Annuities**

You invest now; payments start later.

There are 3 main kinds:

### **1) Fixed Annuity**

* Guaranteed interest rate
* Stable
  **Good for:** Conservative investors

---

### **2) Fixed Indexed Annuity (FIA)**

* Interest linked to an index (S&P 500)
* Has floor (no loss) and cap (limited gain)
  **Good for:** Moderate risk / stability combo

---

### **3) Variable Annuity**

* Invested in mutual-fund-like portfolios
* Value goes up/down with market
* Can add riders for income or death benefits

**Good for:** Higher risk / growth potential

---

## **3. Income Types (Payout Options)**

* **Lifetime income** (for you or joint with spouse)
* **Period certain** (e.g., 10 years guaranteed)
* **Lump sum withdrawal**
* **Systematic withdrawals**

---

## **4. Pros & Cons**

### **Annuity Pros**

* Guaranteed income for life (no fear of outliving money)
* Tax-deferred growth
* Some protection from market losses (indexed annuities)
* Optional riders: long-term care, death benefits, etc.

### **Annuity Cons**

* Can have surrender charges
* Fees (especially variable annuities)
* Limited liquidity
* Can be complex to understand
