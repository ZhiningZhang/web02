Financial Foundations: Life Insurance & Annuities
Managing the Risks of Life and Longevity

The Two Sides of Financial Risk
The Risk of "Dying Too Soon" (Solution: Life Insurance)
The Risk of "Living Too Long" (Solution: Annuities)

What is Life Insurance?
Definition: A contract providing cash to beneficiaries upon death.
Purpose: Income replacement and debt coverage.
Key Question: "If I died yesterday, would my family be okay financially?"

Pros & Cons: Term vs. Whole Life
Term Life (The "Rental"): Affordable, Temporary, No Cash Value.
Whole Life (The "Home"): Expensive, Permanent, Builds Cash Value.
Key Takeaway: Term is for protection; Whole Life is for legacy/equity.

What is an Annuity?
Definition: Converting a lump sum into a stream of income.
Purpose: Longevity Insurance.
Key Feature: Guaranteed paycheck for life.

How Annuities Work
Accumulation Phase: Money grows tax-deferred.
Distribution Phase: The company pays you a monthly income.

Types of Annuities
Fixed Annuities: Safe, guaranteed interest rate.
Variable Annuities: Market-based, higher risk/reward.
Indexed Annuities: Hybrid, offers protection with some upside.

Summary: Which Do You Need?
Life Insurance: If you have dependents or debts.
Annuities: If you want guaranteed retirement income.
