Risk Management and Insurance Planning: Overview

This section tests your knowledge and ability to evaluate and recommend insurance solutions to protect clients from financial loss due to unexpected events, while integrating insurance into a holistic financial plan.

Key Topics Covered

1. Principles of Risk Management

  • Understanding types of risks (personal, property, liability, financial, business risks).
  • Concepts of risk avoidance, reduction, retention, and transfer.
  • Role of insurance as a risk transfer mechanism.

2. Insurance Needs Analysis

  • Analyzing client’s financial situation, goals, and risk exposure.
  • Calculating life insurance needs (human life value approach, capital retention, income replacement).
  • Assessing other insurance needs: disability, long-term care, property & casualty, liability, health.

3. Life Insurance

  • Types of life insurance:

    • Term Life Insurance (pure protection for a term).
    • Whole Life Insurance (permanent, cash value).
    • Universal Life Insurance (flexible premiums and death benefits).
    • Variable Life Insurance (investment component).

  • Policy provisions, riders, and typical contract terms.

  • Taxation of life insurance: premiums, death benefits, cash value growth.

4. Health Insurance

  • Types: individual vs group, HMOs, PPOs, high-deductible health plans, Medicare, Medicaid.
  • Understanding health insurance costs and coverage.
  • Disability insurance basics and integration with Social Security Disability.

5. Long-Term Care Insurance

  • Coverage specifics, benefit triggers, elimination periods, inflation protection.
  • Role in protecting assets and planning for elder care expenses.

6. Property and Casualty Insurance

  • Homeowners, automobile, liability coverage.
  • Understanding limits, deductibles, exclusions, and endorsements.

7. Annuities

  • Fixed, variable, indexed, immediate, deferred annuities.
  • Role in retirement income planning and risk mitigation (longevity, market risk).
  • Fee structures, payout options, tax implications.

8. Insurance Contract Fundamentals

  • Elements of contracts: offer, acceptance, consideration, legal purpose, competent parties.
  • Concepts like insurable interest, utmost good faith, subrogation, indemnity.

9. Insurance Regulation and Ethics

  • Understanding licensing requirements, regulatory environment.
  • Ethical considerations and fiduciary responsibilities in recommending insurance products.

How This Appears on the Exam

  • Expect multiple-choice questions requiring application of concepts — e.g., calculating insurance needs, comparing policy types, recommending solutions based on client profiles.
  • Case-based questions where you evaluate insurance options as part of a broader financial plan.

Suggested Study Focus

  • Master the differences and uses of insurance products.
  • Practice needs analysis and risk evaluation exercises.
  • Understand tax and legal implications.
  • Review common insurance contract terms and policy features.
