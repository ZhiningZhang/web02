The gift tax is a federal tax in the United States on the transfer of money, property, or assets from one person to another without getting something of equal value in return.

  • Who pays it: The giver (donor) usually pays the tax, not the person receiving the gift.
  • Why it exists: To stop people from avoiding estate tax by giving away their wealth before they die.
  • When it applies: Only if your gift is worth more than the annual exclusion amount or if you exceed your lifetime exemption.

  1. Annual Gift Tax Exclusion

    • You can give up to $18,000 per person per year without having to file a gift tax return.
    • Married couples can give $36,000 jointly per recipient.

  2. Lifetime Exemption

    • About $13.61 million over your lifetime (combined with estate tax exemption).
    • Gifts above the annual limit count toward this lifetime amount.

  3. Tax Rate

    • Up to 40% on amounts above the lifetime exemption.
