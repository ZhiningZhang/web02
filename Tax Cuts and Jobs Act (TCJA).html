The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) is a major tax reform law passed in the United States in December 2017. It was signed into law by President Donald Trump and became effective starting in 2018.

🔹 Key Goals:

  • Stimulate economic growth
  • Simplify the tax code
  • Lower tax rates for individuals and businesses

🔑 Key Features of the TCJA:

🧍‍♂️ For Individuals:

  1. Lower Income Tax Rates:

    • Reduced most individual tax brackets (e.g., top rate dropped from 39.6% to 37%).

  2. Standard Deduction Doubled:

    • Single filers: from ~$6,350 to $12,000 (in 2018)
    • Married couples: from ~$12,700 to $24,000

  3. Personal Exemptions Eliminated:

    • Previously, you could deduct ~$4,050 per person.

  4. Child Tax Credit Increased:

    • From $1,000 to $2,000 per child; higher income limits.

  5. State and Local Tax (SALT) Deduction Capped:

    • Deduction limited to $10,000.

  6. Mortgage Interest Deduction Limited:

    • For new mortgages, deduction capped to the first $750,000 of debt.

  7. No More Penalty for Not Having Health Insurance:

    • The individual mandate under the Affordable Care Act was eliminated starting in 2019.

  8. Temporary Provisions:

    • Most individual tax changes are set to expire after 2025 unless extended by Congress.

🏢 For Businesses:

  1. Corporate Tax Rate Cut:

    • From 35% to 21%, permanently.

  2. 100% Bonus Depreciation:

    • Businesses can immediately deduct the full cost of certain assets.

  3. Limits on Interest Deductions:

    • Interest expense deduction generally limited to 30% of EBITDA (or EBIT after 2021).

  4. Shift to Territorial Tax System:

    • Encourages companies to repatriate foreign earnings with a lower tax burden.

  5. Pass-Through Deduction (Section 199A):

    • Many small businesses and partnerships can deduct up to 20% of their qualified business income (with restrictions).

📅 Duration

  • Corporate tax changes: Permanent
  • Individual tax changes: Mostly temporary, expiring after 2025

💬 Summary:

The TCJA was the biggest overhaul of the U.S. tax code in over 30 years. It lowered taxes for most taxpayers and cut corporate rates to encourage investment and repatriation of profits. However, it also increased the federal deficit, and many individual tax benefits are scheduled to end in 2025 unless renewed.
