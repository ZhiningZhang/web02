Certainly! Here’s a detailed list of the major topic areas covered in the CFP Certification curriculum, broken down into their key subtopics. This overview aligns with the CFP Board Principal Knowledge Topics and provides a comprehensive guide for your study plan.

CFP Major Topic Areas & Their Detailed Subtopics

1. Professional Conduct and Regulation

  • CFP Board Code of Ethics and Standards of Conduct
  • Fiduciary duty and ethical responsibilities
  • Client confidentiality and conflicts of interest
  • Regulatory environment (SEC, FINRA, insurance regulations)
  • CFP Board Disciplinary Rules and Procedures
  • Anti-money laundering (AML) and compliance
  • Recordkeeping and documentation standards

2. General Principles of Financial Planning

  • Financial planning process and client engagement
  • Data gathering and analysis
  • Goal setting and prioritization
  • Time value of money concepts
  • Financial statements and budgeting
  • Cash flow management
  • Communication skills and client education
  • Behavior finance and client psychology

3. Education Planning

  • College savings goals and cost projections
  • Financial aid fundamentals
  • Education savings vehicles (529 plans, Coverdell ESAs)
  • Custodial accounts (UGMA/UTMA)
  • Tax considerations of education funding
  • Student loans and repayment strategies

4. Risk Management and Insurance Planning

  • Principles of risk and risk management
  • Insurance needs analysis and gap analysis
  • Life insurance products: term, whole, universal, variable
  • Health insurance: types, Medicaid, Medicare, ACA
  • Disability insurance and Social Security Disability
  • Long-term care insurance
  • Property and casualty insurance
  • Annuities: fixed, variable, indexed, immediate, deferred
  • Insurance contract law and policy provisions
  • Insurance regulation and ethical considerations

5. Investment Planning

  • Investment fundamentals and portfolio theory
  • Asset classes and characteristics (equities, bonds, cash, alternatives)
  • Risk and return concepts
  • Diversification and asset allocation
  • Investment vehicles: mutual funds, ETFs, stocks, bonds, REITs
  • Tax-efficient investing
  • Retirement plan investment options
  • Portfolio management strategies
  • Behavioral biases in investing
  • Performance measurement and benchmarking

6. Tax Planning

  • Federal income tax fundamentals
  • Taxable vs. nontaxable income
  • Filing status and tax brackets
  • Deductions, credits, and exemptions
  • Capital gains and losses
  • Tax treatment of investments and retirement accounts
  • Tax planning strategies (tax loss harvesting, timing income and deductions)
  • Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)
  • Gift and estate tax basics
  • Tax implications of business ownership

7. Retirement Planning

  • Retirement needs analysis and goal setting
  • Social Security benefits and strategies
  • Employer-sponsored retirement plans (401(k), 403(b), pension plans)
  • IRAs (Traditional, Roth, SEP, SIMPLE)
  • Distribution rules and required minimum distributions (RMDs)
  • Retirement income strategies (withdrawal sequencing, annuities)
  • Medicare and health care in retirement
  • Planning for longevity risk and inflation
  • Estate considerations in retirement planning

8. Estate Planning

  • Estate planning process and client engagement
  • Wills and trusts (revocable, irrevocable, testamentary, special purpose)
  • Powers of attorney and advance directives
  • Probate process and avoidance strategies
  • Gift and estate tax fundamentals
  • Strategies to minimize estate and inheritance taxes
  • Charitable giving techniques
  • Wealth transfer planning and generation skipping
  • Trust administration and fiduciary responsibilities
  • Business succession planning
