Absolutely! The third card drawing rules in Baccarat can seem tricky, but they’re fixed and automatic — meaning players don’t decide; the dealer follows these rules exactly.

Third Card Drawing Rules in Baccarat (Punto Banco Version)

Step 1: Check for Natural Win

  • If either Player or Banker has a total of 8 or 9 with their first two cards, no more cards are drawn and the hand ends.

Step 2: Player’s Third Card Rule

  • If Player’s total is 0–5, Player draws a third card.
  • If Player’s total is 6 or 7, Player stands (no third card).

Step 3: Banker’s Third Card Rule

The Banker’s decision depends on both the Banker’s total and whether the Player drew a third card, and if so, what that third card was.

Banker’s Total Player’s Third Card (if any) Banker Action
0–2 Any Banker draws a third card
3 Not 8 Banker draws
4 2–7 Banker draws
5 4–7 Banker draws
6 6–7 Banker draws
7 — Banker stands
  • If Player did not draw a third card, then Banker draws a third card if Banker’s total is 0–5, otherwise stands.

What does this mean?

  • Banker’s decision is more complex because it depends on Player’s third card value.
  • The Banker follows these rules to maximize the chances to get closer to 9.

Example

  • Player’s first two cards total 5 → Player draws a third card (say, 4).
  • Banker’s first two cards total 4 → Banker looks at Player’s third card (4). Since Banker total is 4 and Player’s third card is between 2–7, Banker draws a third card.

Summary of Drawing Rules:

Condition Player Action Banker Action
Player has 6 or 7 Stands (no third card) Draws if Banker total 0–5; else stands
Player has 0–5 Draws third card See table above based on Player’s third card and Banker total
