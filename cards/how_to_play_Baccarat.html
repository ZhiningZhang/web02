What is Baccarat?

Baccarat is a simple comparing card game typically played between two hands: the Player and the Banker. The goal is to bet on which hand will have a total value closest to 9, or if the hands will tie.

Card Values

  • Cards 2 through 9 are worth their face value.
  • 10, Jack, Queen, and King are worth 0 points.
  • Ace is worth 1 point.

How to Calculate Hand Value

Add the values of the two cards together, then take only the last digit of the sum (i.e., drop the tens digit).

Example:

  • Hand with 7 and 8 = 15 → value is 5 (drop the ‘1’).
  • Hand with 3 and 6 = 9 → value is 9.

Game Play

  1. Place Bets Players bet on either:
  • Player hand wins
  • Banker hand wins
  • Tie

  1. Deal Cards Two cards are dealt face up to both the Player hand and the Banker hand.

  2. Natural Win Check If either hand totals 8 or 9 with the first two cards (called a “natural”), the game ends immediately and the winner is declared.

  3. Third Card Rule If no natural win, a third card may be drawn based on a fixed set of rules:

  • If Player’s total is 5 or less, Player draws a third card. Otherwise, Player stands.
  • Banker’s third card rules depend on Banker’s total and the Player’s third card (if drawn). It’s a bit more complex, but the dealer automatically follows the rules.

Winning and Payouts

  • Winning bet on Player hand: Pays 1:1
  • Winning bet on Banker hand: Pays 1:1 minus 5% commission (due to Banker’s slight advantage)
  • Winning bet on Tie: Usually pays 8:1 or 9:1, but has a much higher house edge

Key Points

  • Baccarat is largely a game of chance; players just choose which hand to bet on.
  • The Banker bet is statistically the best bet due to slightly better odds, even after the commission.
  • The Player bet is the next best.
  • Tie bets have a high house edge and are generally not recommended.

Variants of Baccarat

  • Punto Banco: The most common version found in North America and casinos worldwide; completely dealer-driven with no player decisions after bets.
  • Chemin de Fer: A European version where players can make decisions like drawing cards.
  • Baccarat Banque: Another European variant with slightly different dealing rules.
