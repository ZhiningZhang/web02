1. Game Objective & Flow

Blackjack (also called 21) is played against the dealer, not against other players.

Goal:

Have a hand value closer to 21 than the dealer’s, without going over 21.

Basic Round Sequence:

  1. Place Bet – Put chips in the betting circle.

  2. Initial Deal –

    • Players: 2 cards face up.
    • Dealer: 1 card face up (up-card), 1 face down (hole card).

  3. Player Decisions – You can:

    • Hit
    • Stand
    • Double Down
    • Split
    • Surrender (if allowed)

  4. Dealer’s Turn – Dealer reveals hole card, must draw until at least 17.

  5. Outcome & Payout – Compare your hand to dealer’s.

2. Card Values & Key Terms

Card Value
2–10 Face value
J, Q, K 10
Ace (A) 1 or 11 (whichever benefits the hand)

Key Terms:

  • Soft Hand: Contains an Ace counted as 11 (e.g., A+6 = “soft 17”). Flexible — you can hit without busting immediately.
  • Hard Hand: No Ace, or Ace counted as 1 (e.g., 10+7 = “hard 17”).
  • Bust: Hand value over 21 (automatic loss).
  • Blackjack: Ace + 10-value card on initial deal. Pays 3:2 (in good tables).

3. Player Actions in Detail

  1. Hit

    • Ask for another card.
    • Used when your total is low or when dealer’s up-card is strong.

  2. Stand

    • Keep your hand as is.
    • Used when you have a strong total or dealer’s up-card is weak.

  3. Double Down

    • Double your bet, take exactly one more card.
    • Usually best when your starting total is 9, 10, or 11 against a weak dealer card.

  4. Split

    • If you have two cards of the same rank, split into two separate hands.
    • Most strategies recommend always splitting Aces and 8s.
    • Never split 5s or 10s.

  5. Surrender (if available)

    • Forfeit half your bet and end the hand early.
    • Best when your odds are terrible (e.g., hard 16 vs dealer 10).

4. Dealer Rules

Dealers follow strict rules — they cannot use “gut feeling.”

  • Must hit until total is 17 or more.

  • At some tables:

    • S17 = Dealer stands on soft 17 (better for player).
    • H17 = Dealer hits on soft 17 (slightly worse for player).

5. Winning & Payouts

  • Blackjack: Pays 3:2 (bet $10, win $15) — but some casinos pay 6:5 (worse for you).
  • Regular Win: 1:1 payout.
  • Tie (“Push”): Bet returned.
  • Insurance: Offered if dealer’s up-card is Ace — pays 2:1 if dealer has Blackjack. Statistically a bad bet unless counting cards.

6. Table Rules Variations

Casinos can tweak rules to shift the house edge:

  • Deck count: 1–8 decks. Fewer decks = slightly better for player.
  • Dealer hits/stands on soft 17.
  • Double after split allowed or not.
  • Re-splitting Aces allowed or not.
  • Late surrender availability.
  • Payout changes (3:2 vs 6:5).

7. House Edge & Strategy

  • Using Basic Strategy (a decision chart based on your hand vs dealer’s up-card), house edge drops to about 0.5%.
  • Without strategy, it’s around 2–4%.
  • Counting cards can swing the advantage slightly to you, but casinos watch for it closely.
